DTE Duct and Tendon Exposure Duct and tendon exposure refers to the condition where the protective covering (duct) enclosing the prestressing tendon in a post-tensioned concrete structure becomes exposed or damaged, potentially leading to corrosion of the tendon and compromising the structural integrity of the concrete element.
EAE End Anchorage Exposure End anchorage exposure refers to the condition where the anchorage points of prestressing tendons in a post-tensioned concrete structure become exposed or damaged, potentially leading to corrosion of the tendons and compromising the structural integrity of the concrete element. This exposure can occur due to various factors such as inadequate protective measures, construction defects, or environmental influences.
AAP Asbestos Action Plan An asbestos action plan is a strategic document outlining the steps and procedures to be taken by an organization or entity to manage and mitigate the risks associated with asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) in buildings or facilities under their control. It typically includes measures for identification, assessment, monitoring, removal, and disposal of asbestos, as well as protocols for communication, training, and compliance with relevant regulations and guidelines to ensure the safety of occupants and workers.
ACM Asbestos Containing Material Asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) are building materials or products that contain asbestos fibers. Asbestos was commonly used in construction due to its heat resistance, strength, and insulating properties. However, exposure to asbestos fibers can pose serious health risks, including lung diseases such as asbestosis, lung cancer, and mesothelioma. Common ACMs include insulation, roofing materials, floor tiles, ceiling tiles, and various other construction products. Proper identification, handling, and management of ACMs are essential to prevent exposure and protect public health.
ALL Assessment Live Load Assessment live load refers to the dynamic or moving force applied to a structure, such as a bridge or building, during its design or evaluation process. It accounts for the weight of people, vehicles, furniture, or other temporary loads that may be present on the structure at any given time. Engineers consider assessment live loads when determining the structural capacity and safety factors of the design to ensure that the structure can safely support expected loads during its intended use.
